Your task is to implement the 0/1 Knapsack algorithm (each item chosen at most once) and verify its functional correctness in ACSL using Frama-C/WP. Preconditions: You are given two `const uint64_t *` arrays `weights` and `values` of length `n` (with `0 <= n <= 100`) and a capacity `capacity <= 1000`; per-item weights and values are bounded by 1000. Both arrays must be read-valid over `0 .. n - 1`. Requirements: Implement `solve_knapsack_01` returning a `uint64_t` equal to the optimal achievable value. (1) Algorithm: Use the standard 1-D DP `dp[w]` of length `capacity + 1`, iterating items outer and capacity inner (descending so each item is used at most once); the answer is `dp[capacity]`. (2) Functional Correctness: The contract characterises optimality as a pair of postconditions over arbitrary 0/1 strategies, encoded as `int *selected` arrays whose entries are restricted by `is_valid_strategy` to `{0, 1}`. The axiomatic block defines `total_weight(selected, weights, k, n)` and `total_value(selected, values, k, n)` recursively from the right, with base axiom at `k == n` and step axiom for `0 <= k < n`. You must prove: Upper Bound: every valid strategy yields total value `<= \result`; Existence: some valid strategy achieves total value `== \result`. Verification Challenges: (1) Existence Witness: ACSL's `\exists int *selected; ...` requires materialising a witness pointer at the logical level - in practice this means a ghost array reconstructed from DP traceback information, with lemmas tying `dp[i][w]` to the `total_value` of the partial strategy. (2) Upper Bound by Induction on Items: The classical optimality proof inducts over the item index, requiring loop invariants of shape "for all valid strategies over items `[i..n)` with budget `w`, the total value is `<= dp[i][w]`" - quantifier instantiation over the recursive `total_weight`/`total_value` axioms is the main bottleneck. (3) Quantifier Triggers: The axiomatic step axioms must be instantiated at concrete indices; explicit `assert` statements naming the relevant `total_weight(selected, weights, k, n)` and `total_value(...)` terms typically guide WP. ACSL-specific notes: (a) The contract is `assigns \nothing;` - all DP scratch lives in stack-local arrays whose writes are local. (b) No `\separated` clause is needed for the inputs since they are pure reads, but if you allocate a DP buffer you must keep it `\separated` from `weights` and `values` (locals on the stack provide this automatically). (c) `selected[i] != 0` is used in the step axiom rather than restricting `selected` to a Boolean type, so the predicate `is_valid_strategy` enforces `selected[i] == 0 || selected[i] == 1`. (d) `-wp-model Typed` is sufficient; no top-level lemmas are required, but auxiliary axiomatic lemmas about monotonicity of `total_value` over items may speed verification.
