Your task is to implement the Naive Exponentiation Algorithm and verify its functional correctness in Dafny. This algorithm computes the power of an integer using a simple linear iteration. Preconditions: You can assume the input base b and exponent e are non-negative integers (simulating u64). To ensure safety, you may assume that the mathematical result $b^e$ fits within the 64-bit integer range (i.e., $b^e \le \text{0xffff\_ffff\_ffff\_ffff}$). Requirements: Implement the method exponentiation which returns an integer. (1) Functional Correctness: You must define a recursive ghost function spec_pow(b, e) (where $b^0 = 1$ and $b^n = b \times b^{n-1}$) and prove that your implementation returns exactly this value. (2) Safety: You must prove that the multiplication operation never exceeds the 64-bit limit during any iteration. Verification Challenges: (1) Accumulation Invariant: The algorithm runs for e iterations. You must prove the loop invariant that after i iterations, the accumulator variable equals spec_pow(b, i). (2) Monotonicity for Safety: To prove that the intermediate multiplications do not overflow, you must establish the monotonic property of exponentiation. Specifically, you must prove that if the final result $b^e$ fits in the limit (as assumed in the precondition), then any partial result $b^i$ (where $i \le e$) must also fit.
