Your task is to implement and verify a right rotation that fixes a "Double Red" violation in a Left-Leaning Red-Black Tree (LLRBT) in ACSL using Frama-C/WP. Preconditions: `t` is a non-null `LlrbtNode *` satisfying `is_bst(t)`, with a non-null `t->left` that is Red. (Note: the natural double-red precondition `t->left->left != \null && t->left->left->is_red` is not stated in the contract; the rotation is correct on the weaker precondition above as well, so this is an under-specification rather than a defect.) Requirements: Implement `llrbt_rotateright` returning an `LlrbtNode *`. (1) Algorithm: The classical right rotation - the original left child becomes the new root, the original root becomes the new right child, and the new root inherits the colour of the old root while the new right child becomes Red. Internal pointer rewiring: new_root = t->left; t->left = new_root->right; new_root->right = t; new_root->is_red = t->is_red; t->is_red = true. (2) Functional Correctness: The contract requires `\result` non-null, `is_bst(\result)`, set preservation (`in_view(\result, x) <==> in_view{Pre}(t, x)`), `black_height(\result) == \at(black_height(t), Pre)`, `\result->is_red == \at(t->is_red, Pre)`, a non-null right child, and `\result->right->is_red`. STRUCTURAL DEFECT (flagged challenge): As with `llrbt_rotateleft`, the contract pairs `assigns \nothing;` with structural mutation: `\result != t`, `\result->right == \at(t, Pre)`, and the colour fields are toggled. These are jointly unsatisfiable with `assigns \nothing;` in standard ACSL/WP semantics, since either fresh allocation or in-place rewiring constitutes a write. The recommended fix is to weaken to `assigns t->left, t->left->right, t->is_red, t->left->is_red;` with `ensures \result == \at(t->left, Pre);`. As written, the spec is unsatisfiable. Verification Challenges: (1) Structural Defect (above) - blocks any sound in-place implementation. (2) BST Preservation: The rotation preserves BST order because `t->left->val < t->val`. Unfolding `is_bst` recursively and re-establishing it on the new shape requires lemmas about how `in_view` interacts with reattaching `t->left->right` as the new left subtree of `t`. (3) Black-Height Preservation: The new root is Red iff the old root was, and the previously Red `t->left` becomes Red `t` (which is now the right child of the new root). Both subtree paths retain the same Black-node count. The axiom `black_height_def` must be instantiated for both pre- and post-states with the rearranged child pointers. (4) Under-Specified Precondition: Unlike a textbook description, the contract does not require `t->left->left` to be Red; the rotation is mechanically correct without this hypothesis but the resulting tree may still violate the LLRBT colour invariant `satisfies_red_props`. The contract only re-establishes `is_bst` and the black-height equality, not the full `is_llrbt`, which is consistent with this rotation's role as an internal fixup primitive. ACSL-specific notes: (a) The `assigns \nothing;` clause is the load-bearing defect; the simplest fix is to weaken to in-place writes and add `ensures \result == \at(t->left, Pre);`. (b) Same `reads` and recursive-predicate caveats as for `llrbt_rotateleft` apply: manual lemmas tying `is_bst`, `black_height`, and `in_view` across the new and old tree shapes are required. (c) Aliasing: ACSL has no built-in acyclicity for tree structures; ghost preconditions like `\separated(t, t->left, t->left->right)` may be needed to discharge the in-place rewiring soundly. (d) `-wp-model Typed` is sufficient. (e) No top-level lemmas are required by the contract, but a `lemma right_rotation_preserves_bh` companion to the left-rotation lemma is essentially mandatory in the ghost layer.
